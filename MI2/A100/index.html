— 1919 —-
Anna May Wong’s debut movie has an uncredited background role in The Red Lantern. [Photo of Anna May Wong’s debut]

— 1921 —
Lotus Flower from The Toll of the Sea, Anna May Wong’s first leading role. [Photo of The Toll of the Sea]

— 1924 —
A further step from the Chinese community in 1882, the Immigration Act of 1924 extended restrictions to other Asian immigrant groups. The anti-Asian sentiment continued to grow.

— 1930 —
Anti-miscegenation clause banned depictions of interracial relationships. Actors are excluded systematically from the roles of color that have relationships with other roles played by white actors.

— 1937 —
O-Lan from The Good Earth, Anna May Wong was denied to play this role opposite a white male actor cast to play the Chinese male lead. O-Lan was played by a white actress who won the Best Actress Oscar because of that role.

— 1937 —
The Chinese Exclusion Act was repealed, as the U.S. needed to embody an image of fairness and justice.

— 1961 —
Flower Drum Song, is the first major Hollywood movie based on an Asian-American story and main cast by Asian-American actors. Miyoshi Umeki was nominated for Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy.

— 1965 —-
The Immigration and Nationality Act of 1965 passed. Large-scale Chinese immigration occurred.

— 1969 —-
The Equal Employment Opportunity Commission(EEOC) held a one-day hearing in Hollywood. Presented the evidence that people of color were excluded from nearly all jobs in Hollywood.

— 1993 —
Joy Luck Club, is the second movie centered on an Asian-American story since Flower Drum Song 32 years ago. “its depiction of how the brutality of the lives of women in China could continue to influence the lives of their American daughters” - Gene Siskel

— 1994 —
All-American Girl, is the first sitcom in the U.S. featuring a family of Asians. Canceled only after two weeks. “So much emphasis was put on our ethnicity and the fact that we were Asian-American, that took us out of comedy,” - Margaret Cho

— 2001 —
Lucy Liu played in Charlie's Angels and won Best On-Screen Duo from MTV Movie Award.

— 2004 —
Saving Face is a game-changer for both Asian-American and lesbian representation on screen.

— 2015 —
Fresh off the Boat, is the first full Asian set sitcom in 20 years since All-American Girl. It received a 94% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes. Constance Wu has been nominated for 9 awards for her act in it.

— 2018 —
Sandra Oh played a leading role in Killing Eve, and she later won Best Actress – Television Series Drama at the Golden Globe Awards in 2019.

— 2022 —
Everything Everywhere All at Once won 266 awards out of 405 nominations. Michelle Yeoh won the Best Actresses Oscar.
